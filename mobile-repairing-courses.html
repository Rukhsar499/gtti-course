Course Curriculum

In the mobile repairing course, you don’t just observe the repairing process. You do the repairing yourself. You test the components. You identify the problems. You solve them.

The course includes mobile hardware, basic electronics, smartphone technology, software concepts, and problem-solving skills. The aim is straightforward. When a customer brings you a faulty smartphone, you shouldn’t be scared. You should be confident.

The course is designed to prepare students for the workplace, repair shops, or even their own mobile repair business.

Basic Electronics & Mobile Hardware Foundations: Students begin by understanding electricity and components used inside mobile phones.

Tools and Safety Practices: Students learn to use solder rework stations, multimeters, and follow ESD safety during mobile repairing.

Circuit Understanding & Fault Detection: Covers telephone circuits, PCB tracing, fault detection methods, and cordless phone basics.

Smartphone Technology & Mobile Networks: Students learn GPRS, GPS, WAP, Bluetooth, broadband, baseband, and dual-band systems.

Smartphone Systems & Operating Software: Introduces Android and Windows phone systems, camera phones, multimedia phones and Wi-Fi connectivity.

Troubleshooting Skills: Learn how to analyse problems, identify symptoms, and fix issues confidently.

Troubleshooting: Detect and rectify errors in electronic circuits and communication systems.
