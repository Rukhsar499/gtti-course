Course Curriculum

Some careers begin with a passion to serve, rather than a stethoscope, but with a sense of responsibility. The diploma in hospital management course is designed to cover the administration skills required in hospitals, clinics, nursing homes, and hospital organisations. The students are made aware of how the systems work together to provide quality care, efficiency, and compliance.

The course includes knowledge of the hospital industry along with management concepts.

Introduction to Hospital Management: Understanding the fundamentals of hospital management, hospital structure, and departments.

Hospital Systems and Policies: Familiarisation with hospital laws, policies, and systems that govern hospital functioning and patient rights.

Medical Terminology and Communication: Students learn medical terminology and master effective communication.

Financial Management in hospital: Discussion on budgeting, cost control, billing systems, and basic financial planning.

Human Resource Management: Understanding hospital human resource management, recruitment, training, and management while maintaining teamwork and discipline.

Operational Coordination: Focus on coordination between different hospital departments to avoid delays and improve patient satisfaction.

Quality Assurance and Patient Safety: Instructions on ensuring quality care, cleanliness, safety, and improvement.

Legal and Ethical Practices: Understanding ethical responsibilities, patient confidentiality, and legal norms in a hospital environment.

Strategic Planning and Leadership: Develops leadership skills, decision-making, and strategic thinking required for hospital growth and emergencies.
