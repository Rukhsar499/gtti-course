Course Details

Some students in the healthcare sector are interested in how diseases are discovered, how laboratories operate, and how healthcare management is handled. They are looking for a career that involves meaning, discipline, and care.

The healthcare management course offered at GTTI is for students like you. This course combines paramedical science with management knowledge. It assists students in understanding how the healthcare sector is supported by laboratories, diagnostics, communication, and managed systems.

Course Curriculum

We have designed the curriculum of this healthcare services course in a manner that spans over six semesters. The course curriculum includes scientific knowledge, laboratory work, healthcare communication, and management. We provide the students with both technical and organisational knowledge that is required in a healthcare setting.

This course is also beneficial for those who seek long-term development through healthcare administration training. It is quite an easy course to pursue.

Fundamentals of Microbiology: Learn microorganisms, their nature, and their significance in health and disease.

Special Laboratory Techniques: Familiarises the students with laboratory equipment, techniques, and safety procedures in diagnostic laboratories.

General & Human Anatomy and Physiology: Establishes a strong foundation of the human body and the way its various systems work.

Microbial Physiology – Metabolism: Exposes to the growth and survival of microorganisms and their interactions with the environment.

Professional Communication: Enhances the communication skills necessary in the healthcare industry, like reporting and collaboration.

Ecology and Biodiversity: Assists the students in comprehending the significance of environmental balance and its relation to human health.

Pathogenic Microbiology: The students learn about disease-causing microorganisms and their effects on human health.

Environmental Studies: Includes the significance of environmental health, sustainability, and healthcare responsibility.

Clinical Pathology and Biochemistry: AIntroduces the students to diagnostic procedures, biochemical analysis, and interpretation.

Immunology: Understanding the mechanisms of the immune system and the body’s resistance to diseases.

Biochemical Techniques: Practical understanding of biochemical techniques in a laboratory setting.

Histology and Cytology: Understanding tissue and cell structure is essential for diagnostics and pathology.
